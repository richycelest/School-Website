About Me

learning to code:

I started to learn how to program when I was 16 years old. I took computer science courses at my high school learning the basics of Java and JavaScript. As I started to learn, my passion for coding grew aiding the development of my skills and broadening my range of programming languages. I later received my associate degree in computer science at Norwalk Community College and I’m currently pursuing my computer science degree at UConn. I currently can code proficiently in Python, Java, JavaScript, HTML CSS, and C++.

my career:

While pursuing my passion in computer science I stumbled across IT. Building my gaming PC the curiosity of knowing how my computer works and how these complex machines can talk to one another through the network intrigued me. I was able to start my career at Norwalk Community College as an IT student technician. Through this, I was able to build up not only my networking knowledge but also my cybersecurity knowledge as well. At the height of the pandemic, I aided the fight against covid by working with Yale New Haven Health as an IT Technician Specialist. During this time, I was able to build up knowledge of how corporations and companies manage IT networks and cybersecurity. Currently I'm a security network engineer for US Computer Connection where I not only create, and review scripts and databases; I manage network infrastructures for over 100 different companies.

persuing my passion

Due to my experience and love for this field, I have an obligation to give back to the youth and aid them in the nuances of the future. On the weekends I teach kids ages 8-25 programming, robotics, networking, and more. I partnered up with multiple organizations, providing them with the tools and curriculums they need to explore new topics, projects, and ideas.
